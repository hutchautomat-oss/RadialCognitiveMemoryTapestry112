## The Three Fatal Vulnerabilities

   1. The VLM Spatial Acuity Bottleneck (High Risk - Technical)
   VLMs do not read 3D spatial geometry out of a 2D render with millimeter precision. They segment tokens, look at text, and spot macro-patterns. Forcing a VLM to decode semantic meaning from the precise $(x, y, z)$ position of 8,000 coordinates flattened onto an image is unproven. If the model misinterprets a node's position by 5 pixels, it reads a "Fact" as a "Theory." You are swapping token drift for visual rendering/attention drift.
   2. Deterministic Golden-Angle Collision & Chaos (Medium Risk - Algorithmic)
   Meaning is derived strictly from slot index and insertion order. If Client A inserts [A, B, C] and Client B inserts [B, A, C], the exact same semantic items land in totally different geometric slots. Because position is meaning, your database yields two completely different semantic tapestries based entirely on network latency. This breaks Last-Writer-Wins (LWW) convergence; you converge on a shared file, but the file's internal layout loses its semantic cohesion.
   3. The Router Paradox (Medium Risk - Architectural)
   When you scale out to 1,000 tapestries (The "Cortex"), you must route queries to the correct tapestry. If you use a vector search to pick the tapestry, you reintroduce the unbounded drift, bloat, and retraining requirements you built RCMT to avoid. If you use a rigid categorical router, you break cross-domain discovery and semantic fluidity.

## 6. Multimodal Codebook Governance
If sidecar codebooks drift between version updates, your spatial positions instantly lose their validity.
The Failure Mode: Client A updates its audio-to-codebook mapping to v1.1. Client B remains on v1.0. The exact same sound asset now resolves to a completely different coordinate system.
The Solution (The Telecom/Codec Model): Emulate the ITU-T / IETF RFC methodology:

* Codebooks must be immutable, append-only bitstreams.
* Implement strict structural version-keying directly inside the sidecar header.
* If a client encounters an unknown codebook version ID, it must drop the sidecar data entirely and process only the structural coordinate packet.
